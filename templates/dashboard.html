TDS (Total dissolved solids)

Value: {{ tds }}

Total dissolved solids (TDS) is a measure of the total amount of solids, inorganic and organic matter dissolved in water. The TDS of any water is expressed in milligrams per liter (mg/L) or in parts per million (ppm). Definition of TDS Applications of TDS in water quality studies for streams, rivers and lakes. Although TDS is not generally considered a measure of pollution, i.e. not considered to be associated with health effects, it is used as an indicator of the quality characteristics of drinking water and as an aggregate indicator of the presence of a broad range of chemical contaminants.

EC (Electrical Conductivity)

Value: {{ ec }}

EC (Electrical Conductivity) is a measure of a substance's ability to conduct electricity, which is mainly determined by the presence of dissolved ions in a solution. Higher EC values indicate higher concentrations of dissolved substances, while lower EC values indicate purer water with fewer ions.
